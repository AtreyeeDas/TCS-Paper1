    2. Dataset and Pipeline Flow
The Dataset:
We are using the MUCS Hindi-English Code-Switched Dataset, organized in the standard Kaldi academic format (wav.scp, segments, text). It contains complex, intra-sentential language switches.
How it Passes Through the Pipeline:
    1. Our mucs_slicer.py script reads the Kaldi files, loads massive raw audio recordings, and uses the timestamps to slice out 3,136 perfect sentence-level ground-truth .wav files.
    2. In run_ablation.py, the corresponding text transcript is converted into an IPA phoneme bottleneck.
    3. A reference audio prompt is passed to the ECAPA-TDNN encoder to extract the target voice.
    4. The IPA text and Voice Embedding are passed into the frozen XTTS-v2 API to generate the synthetic waveform.
    5. The waveform passes through the Acoustic Guardrail to trim trailing silence/noise.
    6. The final output is compared against the actual MUCS ground-truth audio slice for evaluation.
    7. Observations and Root Cause Analysis
    • Our terminal logs revealed several critical behaviors. While some validated our theories, others revealed the limitations of our current "frozen model" simulation.
      
Observation 1: Stable Voice Cloning
    • Data: SIM-R remained rock-solid at ~0.64–0.65 across all arms.
    • Meaning: Stage 2 works perfectly. The system can successfully clone a voice zero-shot, regardless of the text or ablation arm.
Observation 2: The Guardrail Fix
    • Data: WER improved dramatically when the guardrail was fixed (Arm 1) compared to when it was removed (Arm 2).
    • Meaning: Stage 5 is mathematically validated. Removing it allows the model to output trailing babble, which Whisper reads as errors. The guardrail cleans the edges effectively.
Observation 3: The WER Inversion (The IPA Mismatch)
    • Data: Arm 4 (Without IPA Unification) yielded a better WER (48%) than the Full System (51%).
    • Root Cause: We are feeding raw IPA symbols (e.g., "ʃ", "ʒ") into a frozen XTTS text encoder that was trained strictly on standard Latin/Devanagari characters. Because it does not natively understand IPA, it hallucinates acoustic babble.
Observation 4: The Extremely High MCD (~700 dB)
    • Data: MCD remains mathematically invalid.
    • Root Cause: This is directly tied to Observation 3. Because the model doesn't understand our IPA tokens, it is generating babble. Mel-Cepstral Distortion measures the physical shape of an audio wave. Even with DTW temporal alignment, comparing human speech to AI-generated babble results in a massive Euclidean distance penalty.
